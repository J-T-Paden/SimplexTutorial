Preparing and Adding Spline

Airlock

  1. Measure from the top inside edge of the frame to the top of the crossbar.
  2. Measure from the bottom inside edge of the frame to the bottom of the crossbar.
  3. Grab a piece of spline, either a scrap piece longer than the taken measurement, or a new piece.

    4. Saw directions

    To cut 45° angle, rotate the saw blade to your left, so the blade is to the left of 90° miter cut angle or the 0° miter saw angle. /

    To cut a 135° angle, rotate the saw blade to your right, so the blade is to the right of the 90° miter cut angle or the 0° miter saw angle. \

    Ends

    1. First cut the right side of the spline piece at a 45° angle
    2. Measure from the right side to the end of your measurement and mark the spline. Extend the mark across the spline at the correct angle if necessary.
    3. Cut the spline at your mark at a 135° angle, opposite angle as step 1, cut an extra 1/16" - 1/8" longer than your measurement to not accidentally cut too short.
